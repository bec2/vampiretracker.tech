Gorbals Southern Necropolis

In 1957, hundreds of schoolchildren swarmed into the Southern Necropolis cemetery, ready to hunt. What were they hunting? Naturally, a vampire.

Two young boys had recently gone missing in the area, and the children believed that a vampire with ‘iron teeth’ had made them into a snack. The children were armed with stakes and knives, ready to get their revenge on the beast, who was apparently seven feet tall. A survivor of this tale from among the rabble, Ronnie Sanderson, reported: ‘I was there. I was in the graveyard when I was eight years old. I’ve been telling my wife about the vampire for years and she’s never believed me’.

Parents and preachers alike were shocked by the children’s behaviour in something akin to the Satanic Panic that once struck the US, and ultimately blamed the import of American comic books for the ‘mass hysteria’.
