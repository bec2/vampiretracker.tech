Alnwick Castle


A medieval chronicler wrote of a frightening vampire that stalked these castle grounds in the 1100s. The beast itself was rumoured to be one of the castle’s old owners, who rose from his shallow grave at night to attack townspeople.


The chronicler, William de Newburgh, was a seasoned documenter of the paranormal. He did, however, disagree with the assumption that the vampire was a lord of the castle, instead attributing sightings to an undead retainer of the lord. It is theorised by some that he proposed this to avoid offending the family who still owned the castle.


An outbreak of plague in the local area was attributed to the vampire’s curse, so much so that the townspeople formed a ‘pitchfork mob’, storming the grounds and recovering the casket from its resting place for a hasty cremation. Upon retrieval of the body, the accounts speak of it bleeding fresh blood when stabbed with a wooden stake, as if it has been feeding on people recently. This further spurred on the frantic, plague-ridden mob in their effort to burn the remains. So much for self isolating, I suppose.
